## The Foundation: Root Cause Medicine

Symptoms are the body's language. They are not the enemy. They are communication. A fever is not a disease — it is the immune system fighting one. Pain is not a malfunction — it is a signal that something needs attention. Depression is not a serotonin deficiency — it is often a downstream consequence of gut dysfunction, nutrient depletion, chronic stress, or toxic burden.

Conventional medicine has been extraordinarily successful at emergency care — acute trauma, infection, surgical intervention. But it struggles with chronic disease because it fundamentally treats symptoms rather than causes. A person with hypothyroidism who is given Synthroid has been given medication for a symptom (low thyroid hormone) without any investigation into WHY the thyroid is not producing adequate hormone.

My approach is to ask why. Why is the thyroid failing? Why is the gut inflamed? Why is the body holding weight? Why are the hormones imbalanced? When we find the answer to why, we can address the cause rather than manage the consequence.

## The Four Root Causes I Investigate

In my clinical experience, the vast majority of patients with chronic health problems — and particularly those who cannot lose weight — have one or more of four underlying issues:

Thyroid dysfunction: The master metabolic regulator is not working properly. This is often missed because standard testing only looks at TSH. Full panel evaluation including Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3, and antibodies tells a completely different story.

Gut dysfunction: The microbiome, intestinal lining, digestive enzymes, stomach acid, and gut motility are compromised. This affects every other system — nutrient absorption, immune function, neurotransmitter production, hormone detoxification, and inflammation levels throughout the body.

Hormone imbalance: Cortisol, estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, insulin, and leptin are out of balance. These hormones regulate fat storage, fat burning, appetite, energy, mood, and metabolism. When they are disrupted, the body's signals become confused.

Toxic burden: The cumulative load of heavy metals, environmental chemicals, mold toxins, pharmaceutical residues, and other poisons has overwhelmed the body's detoxification capacity. The body stores these toxins in fat tissue as a protective mechanism, making fat loss biologically impossible until the burden is addressed.

## My Approach to Conventional Medicine

I respect conventional medicine for what it does well. I have no interest in discrediting good science or dismissing the value of pharmaceutical intervention when it is genuinely necessary. A patient having a heart attack needs emergency medicine, not herbs.

What I challenge is the application of pharmaceutical and surgical intervention to conditions that are fundamentally nutritional, environmental, and lifestyle-based. Autoimmune disease, metabolic syndrome, hormonal dysfunction, chronic pain — these conditions have root causes that can be investigated and addressed. Covering them with medication does not fix them. It maintains function while the underlying problem progresses.

I also challenge the narrowness of conventional diagnostic criteria. "Normal" on a lab test means "within the range we see in the population we tested." It does not mean optimal. The population we are testing is sick. A TSH of 3.5 is considered normal; in my clinical experience, a patient with a TSH of 3.5 will feel significantly better with a TSH of 1.5. These distinctions matter.
