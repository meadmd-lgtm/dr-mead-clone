# Detox Protocol — Dr. Mead

## Why Fat Holds Toxins

The body is brilliantly designed for survival. When it is overwhelmed with toxins it cannot safely eliminate — heavy metals, pesticides, plasticizers, solvents, mold toxins — it packages them in fat tissue to protect vital organs. The liver, kidneys, brain, and heart are shielded by surrounding toxins in adipose tissue where they cause less immediate damage.

This is the most important thing I tell weight loss patients: your fat may literally be protecting your life. The body will not release fat that is serving a protective function until it has a safe way to eliminate the toxins stored in it. This is why detox must precede and accompany any serious weight loss effort. You cannot diet your way out of a toxin storage problem.

When someone loses weight rapidly without supporting detox pathways, they can actually feel worse — they are releasing stored toxins back into circulation faster than the body can eliminate them.

## Stop Toxification First

Before you can detox, you must stop adding to the toxic burden. There is no point in draining a sink while the faucet is still running.

First steps to reduce ongoing toxic load:
- Switch to filtered water. Remove chlorine, fluorine, heavy metals, and pharmaceutical residues. Under-sink reverse osmosis or a high-quality carbon filter are my recommendations.
- Eliminate plastic food and beverage containers. Xenoestrogens from plastics are endocrine disruptors that contribute to hormone-driven weight gain. Glass, stainless steel, or ceramic only.
- Switch to natural personal care products. The skin is the largest organ and absorbs everything applied to it. Conventional deodorants, lotions, shampoos, and cosmetics are loaded with parabens, phthalates, and aluminum.
- Eat organic as much as possible, especially the dirty dozen produce items. Glyphosate and other pesticides disrupt the gut microbiome, damage the liver, and accumulate in tissue.
- Address mold in the home environment. Mold toxins (mycotoxins) are among the most potent poisons affecting the brain and metabolism.

## The Beet Test — Assessing Liver and Transit Function

Before beginning any detox protocol, I assess liver and bowel function. A simple beet test reveals transit time and gives a basic indication of liver detox capacity.

Protocol: Eat a significant portion of red beets (cooked or raw). Note the time you ate them. Watch for the appearance of red-tinged stool or urine.

Urine turns pink: If urine turns pink shortly after eating beets, this indicates the liver is not efficiently processing the pigment betacyanin. Healthy livers convert it before it reaches the bloodstream. Pink urine suggests compromised liver detox capacity and means detox support must be gentle.

Stool transit: Note when you first see red in the stool. Healthy transit time is 12 to 24 hours. Over 24 hours indicates constipation and sluggish elimination — the colon must be addressed before liver or deeper detox begins.

## The 4-Phase Detox Sequence

Detoxification must happen in the correct order. Mobilizing toxins before the elimination pathways are open creates a dangerous traffic jam — toxins circulate and cause more damage than if you had done nothing. The sequence is non-negotiable.

Phase 1 — Colon: The sewer system must be clear first. A constipated colon during a liver cleanse means bile loaded with toxins gets reabsorbed. I use a gentle colon cleanse protocol (see colon protocol) before moving to phase 2. Bowel movements should be at least once daily, ideally two to three times, before proceeding.

Phase 2 — Liver and Gallbladder: The liver is the primary detox organ. It processes fat-soluble toxins and packages them for bile excretion. Support the liver with milk thistle (silymarin), dandelion root, burdock root, and N-acetyl cysteine. Coffee enemas (a traditional naturopathic practice) are excellent for opening bile flow and supporting glutathione production. Gallbladder flushes using olive oil and citrus can help clear bile sludge.

Phase 3 — Blood and Lymphatic System: The lymphatic system has no pump — it depends on movement, breathing, and manual stimulation. Dry brushing, rebounding on a mini-trampoline, deep diaphragmatic breathing, and cold/hot hydrotherapy all move lymph. Herbs for lymphatic support include cleavers, red clover, and ocotillo.

Phase 4 — Cellular and Heavy Metal Detox: This is the deepest level and should only be approached after the elimination pathways are fully open and functioning. Cellular detox involves binders (activated charcoal, zeolite, chlorella) to capture and remove mobilized toxins from the gut. Heavy metal detox may use modified citrus pectin, DMSA (under clinical supervision), or high-dose chlorella and cilantro protocols.

## Fulvic and Humic Minerals

One of the most powerful tools in my detox and nutritional toolkit is fulvic acid and humic minerals. These are organic compounds produced by the decomposition of plant matter in soil. They are among the most complex and biologically active substances in nature.

Fulvic acid does several critical things:
- It chelates (binds) heavy metals and escorts them out of the body
- It dramatically increases cellular absorption of minerals and nutrients
- It supports mitochondrial function and cellular energy production
- It modulates immune function and reduces inflammation
- It carries oxygen to tissues

Most people are profoundly mineral deficient because our soils are depleted. Fulvic minerals replenish trace minerals at the cellular level in a form the body can actually use. I consider this a foundational supplement for virtually every patient.

I recommend liquid fulvic minerals added to water daily, away from other supplements and medications since it can increase their absorption. Start with small doses and work up.
